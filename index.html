In successful rare disease trials,
patient-centricity and site-centricity go hand
in hand. Here’s what you need to achieve both.
Read the article   
 
 
Developing a successful rare disease trial differs greatly from running trials in other therapeutic areas. Identifying and recruiting patients is more difficult and time-consuming. Site logistics, administrative burdens, and - most importantly - patient needs are unique. Parexel’s Site Alliance program develops long-term strategic relationships with the most experienced research and healthcare institutions, leading to successful project delivery. Parexel Site Alliance Manager Agnieszka Gackowska outlines best practices for developing a rare disease trial that reduces the trial burden of both patients and the site staff.
